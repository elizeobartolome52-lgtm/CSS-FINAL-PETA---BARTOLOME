Marikina flood control earns praise

Public Works and Highways Secretary Vince Dizon has lauded the flood control initiatives of Marikina City, describing them as a model that other local governments can replicate in addressing flooding challenges.

During his assessment, Dizon highlighted key projects such as the Marikina River channel improvement, slope protection and revetment works, and the expansion of drainage capacity through the installation of giant culvert pipes along Sumulong Highway.

He noted that the city’s integrated approach—combining river rehabilitation, structural flood control measures, and well-planned drainage systems—has significantly strengthened Marikina’s resilience against flooding.

According to the DPWH chief, this comprehensive strategy demonstrates how infrastructure planning and environmental management can work together to reduce flood risks and protect communities, especially in highly urbanized areas.

Dizon emphasized that the “Marikina experience” offers valuable lessons for other cities and municipalities, urging them to adopt similar long-term and coordinated solutions to improve flood management nationwide.
