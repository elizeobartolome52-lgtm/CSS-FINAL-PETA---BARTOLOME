Marikina marks Women’s Day with purple car-free Sunday

The City Government of Marikina joined the celebration of International Women's Day by turning its Car-Free Sundays event purple in honor of women’s strength, leadership, and contributions to the community.

Maan Teodoro said the initiative reflects the city’s commitment to creating an environment where women feel respected, safe, and empowered.

According to the mayor, Marikina is often described as a city that “feels like home,” emphasizing that a true home is a place where women are valued and given opportunities to thrive.

The Car-Free Sunday activity, which allows residents to enjoy city streets without vehicles, also serves as a platform to highlight advocacy efforts and community participation.

City officials reiterated that Marikina will continue promoting safe, inclusive, and accessible public spaces that support equality and community well-being for all residents.
