The U in Marikina Stands for Utopia

Is there a letter U in the name Marikina? No, and that's the current state of our city. But it doesn't have to be that way forever. There are several ways that we could improve our city, namely: investing towards the youth.

As a famous saying goes, Kabataan ang pag-asa ng bayan, but what is that hope in the face of dipilitated facilities where students have to cram for short hours in the name of education. What makes the people in power think that children can actually learn in such environment? The government laments the decreasing competencies of students, then maybe education funds for the country shouldn't go towards the pockets of politicians who didn't give a single thought towards the betterment of students.

Imagine a city of Marikina where students flourish under the government who act as they are expected. Students wouldn't dread going to dipilitated classrooms because they actually have a supportive environment where they wouldn't be worried about getting a heat stroke, nor accidentally hitting their environment because the classrooms are too cramped. Students will be able to actually do their laboratory assignments because there is an actual laboratory to use. Maybe this way, we could produce the next greatest scientist. Unfortunately, they are stuck on watching YouTube for their worksheets because the school doesn't have funding for the science classes' experiments.
