Silence won't make any difference: A call for Environmental Change in Marikina

"Munting Basura, Ibulsa muna" such slogans are plastered all over our city, and students dance to it every flag ceremony. Lectures about taking care of the environment always call for the students and citizens to pick up their slack and care for the environment. But what of the government? For all the efforts citizens put in towards our city, since when did they uphold their bargain of accessible side walks, protections against the typhoons, and actual Disaster risk management for its citizens? Where is the taxpayers money going to?

In every sidewalk, animal litters stain the streets, if you could actually use the street. Most of the time, electrical posts stand as an obstacle in the sidewalk, causing the pedestrian to walk towards the car filled road. Not only feces and posts litter the sidewalk, unpicked up trash bags by the garbage truck litters our sidewalk, made worse by the fact that it's near the public market where common Marikeños buy their food. When they talk about noise pollution, they criticize the citizens for riding vehicles, but turn a blind eye when the sidewalks they nag us to use aren't accessible due to the neglect.

Let this essay be a reminder that caring for the environment goes both ways. While it is true that there are some citizens who pretend pollution isn't a thing, the government shouldn't forget that they are the ones who hold the power, meaning they have bigger responsibilities that they need to fulfill. Responsibilities that are seemingly put in a back burner.
